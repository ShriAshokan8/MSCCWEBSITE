Unity Week Schedule

View and manage the schedule for the overall event.

All club activities occur at lunchtime from 12:40 to 13:20.

Film Club

Watch short films from different countries, followed by quizzes and optional group discussions.

Games Club

Play traditional games from around the world with trivia about their cultural origins; optional mini-tournament.

Coding & Micro:bit Club

Code digital greeting cards with Micro:bits saying “hello” in different languages and showing cultural symbols.

Book Club

Read and discuss folk tales and short stories from different cultures with creative activities like rewriting stories.

Pride Club

Create a display about global LGBTQ+ figures and stories, with quotes, flags, and timelines.

Anime Club

Set up a display with anime history, character art, and an anime quiz.

Geography Eco Club

Make crafts inspired by Indigenous cultures (e.g., dot painting, weaving) and explore cultural links to the environment.

Board Games Club

Introduce global board games and trivia; learn about the history and culture behind each.

Portuguese Club

Learn traditional Portuguese dances, listen to cultural music, and explore Portuguese art and traditions.

Anime Club

Explore anime's global impact through posters, innovation-themed quizzes, and scavenger hunts.

Reading & Puzzles Club

Solve puzzles, riddles, and trivia from different cultures; contribute to a shared “puzzle wall.”

Japanese Club

Set up a cultural display about Japanese life, traditions, and inventions with videos and fun quizzes.

Company of Entrepreneurs

Share stories of entrepreneurs from various cultures and present modern ideas inspired by tradition.

Science for Life Club

Host a global science and invention quiz (e.g., algebra, astronomy, medicine) with student-led presentations.

Choir

Perform multicultural songs, prepared throughout the week, with cultural background info included.

Feel Good Fridays Club

Create a “Wall of Kindness and Culture,” hand out culture awards, and celebrate learning from the week.
