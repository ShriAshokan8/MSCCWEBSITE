5-Day MSC Competition Plan

Day 1: Maths Day

  • 9:30 AM - 9:45 AM: Opening Ceremony - Welcome speech and introduction to the competition.
  • 9:45 AM - 10:45 AM: Maths Quiz - A mix of arithmetic, geometry, problem-solving, and logic questions. Teams compete to score the most points.
  • 10:45 AM - 11:00 AM: Short Break
  • 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM: Maths Project Presentations - Teams present a maths-related project (e.g., real-world applications, creative problem-solving). Judges score the presentations.

Day 2: Science Day

  • 9:30 AM - 10:30 AM: Science Quiz - Questions cover topics like biology, physics, chemistry, and environmental science.
  • 10:30 AM - 10:45 AM: Short Break
  • 10:45 AM - 12:15 PM: Science Project Presentations - Teams show experiments or projects related to science (e.g., eco-friendly designs, science experiments).

Day 3: Computing Day

  • 9:30 AM - 10:30 AM: Computing Quiz - Questions about coding, hardware, algorithms, and technology.
  • 10:30 AM - 10:45 AM: Short Break
  • 10:45 AM - 12:15 PM: Computing Project Presentations - Teams present computing-related projects like apps, websites, or future technology ideas.

Day 4: Combined Day

  • 9:30 AM - 11:00 AM: Combined Quiz - Teams answer questions blending Maths, Science, and Computing.
  • 11:00 AM - 11:15 AM: Short Break
  • 11:15 AM - 12:30 PM: Combined Project Presentations - Teams demonstrate how all three subjects (Maths, Science, and Computing) work together to solve real-world problems.

Day 5: Finale and Awards Day

  • 9:30 AM - 10:30 AM: Fun STEM Activity or Workshop - Example: A guest speaker, hands-on STEM challenges, or a STEM career showcase.
  • 10:30 AM - 10:45 AM: Short Break
  • 10:45 AM - 12:00 PM: Awards Ceremony - Announce winners for each subject quiz, best projects, and combined scores. EG Prizes: Trophies, medals, and certificates.
  • 12:00 PM - 12:30 PM: Closing Remarks - Thank participants, judges, and organizers. Group photos.

How to Manage Time for 20 Teams:

  • Project Presentations: Each team gets 6 minutes (4 minutes to present + 2 minutes for Q&A).
  • Split presentations: These will be spread across the first four days.
  • Judging: Judges evaluate projects on the same day to avoid delays.
